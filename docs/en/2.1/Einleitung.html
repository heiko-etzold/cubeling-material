Cubeling – Teachers’ Guide

Introduction

Wooden cubes fascinate children and adults alike – even before doing any math with them. This enthusiasm is worth taking advantage of! Especially in geometry lessons, but also beyond that, there are numerous/various occasions to use cubes to approach mathematical topics.

Of course this works with actual wooden cubes, pretty well actually. Should experiences like that really be replaced by an app? No! But those experiences can be wonderfully expanded upon. Here a few special features come into play that only digital tools can provide. These lead to further spatial experiences or more opportunities to support and challenge your students.

The Cubeling app covered here is available free of charge for iOS and macOS devices (i.e. iPads, Macs, and, to a limited extent, iPhones) under https://apps.apple.com/de/app/klötzchen/id1027746349.

About this “book”

The first chapter of this document will provide instructions as to how to use the app itself.

The following chapters will include possible applications for teaching mathematics – on one hand with a focus on spatial imagination, on the other on programming in connection with the building of cube structures.

This is not a fixed book. Over time, things will be added or sometimes changed. We will also gladly take up any suggestions in order to share more ideas on how to use the Cubeling app in the classroom.

The current version of this book refers to the Cubeling app of the version 7.1, but many functions are also available with earlier versions. The book, especially chapter 3, builds on a previous, two-part guide (view Etzold & Janke (2018a) und Etzold & Janke (2018b)).

The book is written in a way that makes it easy to read through front to back, but it is intended to also function as a quick reference work.

Tasks for students can be found in the colored boxes.

Gray boxes provide further hints that can, for example, be used in class discussions.

In addition, didactic comments on individual topics are scattered throughout the book. Tips on which materials are suitable for certain teaching situations and which settings are needed in the Cubeling app are also given.

Additional material, like the compilation of all tasks and worksheets referred to in the individual sections, can be found at the end of the book.
